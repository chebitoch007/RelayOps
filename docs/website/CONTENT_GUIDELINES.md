# Content Guidelines

## Purpose

The voice, tone, and honesty rules for all RelayOps content — website copy, outreach emails, demo documentation, and any public-facing material.

---

## The Non-Negotiable Rule

**Never fabricate anything presented as real.**

This means:
- No invented client logos or names
- No fabricated testimonials or quotes
- No made-up metrics ("47 workflows automated this week")
- No case studies based on hypothetical clients
- No revenue figures that don't exist

If credibility does not yet exist, create it through real engineering work. A working demo system with real architecture documentation is more credible than five invented testimonials.

**One exception:** Illustrative/conceptual examples are acceptable — but they must be **clearly labeled.** The `/demo-projects` page uses "Illustrative Example" labels on metrics and outcomes. The `DemoSection.tsx` homepage section uses the same approach. This is the correct pattern. Do not remove these labels.

---

## Voice

RelayOps writes like one competent founder talking to another — not like a marketing team writing to an audience.

**Characteristics:**
- Direct. Say what you mean in the fewest words.
- Curious, not salesy. Ask questions. Don't push.
- Honest about what the business is (one person, pre-revenue, no clients yet) rather than inflating it.
- Technical without being jargon-heavy. Engineers speak precisely — that means choosing clear words, not impressive ones.

---

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Homepage hero / CTA | Confident, outcome-focused. "Stop losing deals to slow follow-up." |
| Founder / About section | Personal, direct, honest about stage of business |
| Demo Projects page | Technical, detailed, proof-oriented |
| Cold outreach emails | Founder-to-founder, conversational, curious — not a pitch |
| Follow-up emails | Brief, adds new value, never pressures |

---

## Writing Rules

**Write outcomes, not features.**
- ❌ "We build AI chatbots"
- ✅ "Respond to new leads in under 2 seconds, automatically"

**Write for one reader, not an audience.**
The primary reader of every website page is a skeptical agency founder who just received a cold email from Brian. Write to that person.

**Don't explain AI.**
The target customer already knows what AI is. Don't spend words on it. Spend words on the business problem it solves.

**Be specific.**
"5–7 days from deposit to live system" is better than "fast delivery." "80–150 words" is better than "concise."

**One idea per paragraph.**
If a paragraph is doing more than one job, split it.

**Don't use these phrases:**
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Revolutionizing"
- "Game-changing"
- "Leverage" (as a verb)
- "Seamlessly"
- "At the forefront"
- "I hope this finds you well"
- "Just following up"

---

## What to Do When Credibility Doesn't Exist Yet

The website launched with zero clients and zero testimonials. The correct response is **not** to invent credibility. The correct responses, in order of preference:

1. **Show real engineering.** The demo system architecture diagram, the n8n workflow, the Gemini integration — these are real and can be shown. Use them.
2. **Label illustrative examples correctly.** The demo metrics on the homepage and `/demo-projects` are labeled "Illustrative Example" — keep those labels.
3. **Be honest about stage.** The About section states plainly that RelayOps is a new agency. This is not a weakness — it is positioned as an advantage (founder directness, no overhead, honest pricing).
4. **Wait until real results exist.** Real client case studies will be added when a real client grants permission. Until then, the placeholders stay as placeholders.

---

## AI Involvement

RelayOps must not imply that its website, demos, or deliverables were built without AI assistance when they were. AI collaboration is a normal part of how the business operates — it should not be hidden or misrepresented.

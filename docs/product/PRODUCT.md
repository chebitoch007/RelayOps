# Product

## Purpose

Define the services RelayOps sells today, how they are delivered, and the long-term product direction.

---

## Background

RelayOps is a services business — it does not sell software. Each engagement is a custom-built automation system, delivered in 5–7 days, designed around the specific bottleneck a client describes on a discovery call.

"Product" in this context means: the standardized, repeatable systems Brian can build reliably, and how they are packaged and delivered.

---

## Current State

Pre-revenue. No systems have been delivered to a paying client. One internal demo system (Lead Qualification) has been built as a sales asset.

---

## Service Offerings

### 1. Lead Follow-Up Automation

**Problem it solves:** Agency receives inbound inquiry (form submission, email, ad click). Someone has to manually follow up. That person is usually the founder. It takes hours. The lead goes cold or goes to a competitor who replied faster.

**What RelayOps builds:** An automated system that responds in seconds, acknowledges the inquiry, sends a relevant qualifying question, and routes the lead based on their response — all without the founder touching anything.

**Delivery components:**
- Intake form or webhook trigger (existing form or new)
- AI-powered initial response (natural language, personalized to the lead's input)
- Qualification logic (branch: qualified / needs more info / not qualified)
- Routing: qualified leads → instant Calendly booking link; others → nurture or discard
- Data logged to a central sheet or CRM
- Notification to the founder on qualified leads

**Price range:** $300–600

---

### 2. AI Chatbot (Website)

**Problem it solves:** Website visitors have questions. Nobody answers them in real time. Leads leave without converting.

**What RelayOps builds:** A trained, on-brand chatbot that handles first-touch conversations, answers common questions, qualifies the visitor, and routes to a booking or contact form.

**Price range:** $400–800

---

### 3. WhatsApp Automation

**Problem it solves:** Clients communicate via WhatsApp. Responding manually at scale is unsustainable.

**What RelayOps builds:** AI-powered WhatsApp responses using the WhatsApp Business API, with qualification logic and automated follow-up sequences.

**Price range:** $300–500

---

### 4. Appointment Booking System

**Problem it solves:** Booking discovery calls requires back-and-forth emails. Founders spend time scheduling instead of selling.

**What RelayOps builds:** A fully automated booking flow — AI qualifies the lead, sends the Calendly link, handles reminders, logs the booking, and sends confirmation messages. Integrates with existing calendar.

**Price range:** $400–700

---

### 5. Combined / Multi-Automation System

**Problem it solves:** Multiple manual processes exist; solving just one leaves gaps.

**What RelayOps builds:** Two or more of the above integrations, architected to work together as a single coherent system.

**Price range:** $700–1,500

---

## What Is Not Offered (Currently)

- Ongoing monthly retainers (not discussed, not offered yet)
- SaaS products or subscription tools
- Generic "AI strategy" consulting without building anything
- Social media automation, content creation, or marketing execution
- Systems built on tools the client must manage (unless explicitly agreed)

---

## Delivery Standard

- **Deposit:** 50% to begin
- **Delivery:** 5–7 business days from deposit
- **Handoff package:** Working system + Loom walkthrough + one-page explainer doc
- **Balance:** 50% on delivery and satisfaction
- **Post-delivery:** 1 round of tweaks included (scope to be defined per project)

---

## Long-Term Product Direction

Not formally defined. The current focus is on closing the first client. Potential future directions — none approved:

- Standardized "packages" with fixed components and prices (reduces custom quoting overhead)
- A productized recurring tier for clients who want ongoing monitoring/maintenance
- Specialization in one automation type (e.g., lead qualification exclusively) once there's data on which sells fastest
- Hiring to expand capacity once revenue justifies it

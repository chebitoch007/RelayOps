# Business Model

## Purpose

Define what RelayOps sells, to whom, at what price, and the strategic logic that underlies each choice.

---

## Background

RelayOps was created on a deliberate strategic premise: a service business reaches revenue in weeks; a SaaS product takes months before the first dollar. With limited capital, no existing audience, and a single founder in university, the service model is the only viable path to validating that this business can work before scaling it.

---

## Current State

**Business type:** Done-for-you AI automation agency (services, not software)

**Legal/operational form:** Solo founder, single-person operation (Brian Chebitoch)

**Stage:** Pre-revenue. Zero clients closed. Zero testimonials. In active outreach.

**Primary revenue motion:** Outbound cold email → discovery call → proposal → deposit → build → delivery

---

## What RelayOps Sells

RelayOps builds custom automation systems that eliminate specific, expensive manual processes for agencies and service businesses. Current service offerings:

| Service | What it does | Price range |
|---|---|---|
| Lead follow-up automation | Responds to inbound leads in seconds, qualifies via AI, books to calendar | $300–600 |
| AI chatbot (website) | Handles first-touch conversations, qualifies, routes | $400–800 |
| WhatsApp automation | AI-powered WhatsApp responses, qualification, follow-up | $300–500 |
| Appointment booking system | Automated scheduling without human intervention | $400–700 |
| Combined/multi-automation | Two or more integrations in a single build | $700–1,500 |

**General pitch price range used in outreach messaging:** $300–800 per project.

**What is NOT sold:** Generic AI tools, SaaS subscriptions (no recurring pricing model exists yet), consulting retainers (not discussed), or pre-built templates.

## Who It's Sold To

### Primary ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

- **Marketing/digital agencies and service businesses**
- **5–20 employees** — large enough to have real lead volume and budget, small enough that the founder still reads their own email
- **Founder-led** — the founder personally handles sales/discovery calls, which is both the bottleneck RelayOps sells the fix for, and the reason a single email can land a deal without procurement bureaucracy
- **Growing or actively hiring** — signals capacity strain, making automation a timely solution
- **Already sells lead generation or growth services to their own clients but hasn't automated their own intake** — the "physician, heal thyself" hook used in nearly every outreach email
- **English-speaking markets** — US, UK, Canada, Australia (removes localization friction)

### Secondary / Undecided Niche

Individual real estate agents, brokerages, and teams surfaced accidentally during Apollo.io prospecting. Strong fit signal (real estate agents are notorious for slow lead response), but a different buyer psychology than marketing agencies. Approximately 14+ prospects have been sourced but not contacted. **This expansion has not been approved.** See `DECISIONS.md` #2.

---

## Why This Customer

The "physician, heal thyself" dynamic is the structural insight driving all targeting: marketing agencies are sophisticated about lead generation for clients but have often applied no automation to their own intake. That irony is credible, verifiable (it can be spotted from their website), and creates a natural opening without needing to explain what automation is.

---

## Deal Structure

- **Payment:** 50% deposit to begin, 50% due on delivery/satisfaction
- **Delivery target:** 5–7 days from deposit receipt
- **Scope management if pushed back on price:** Reduce scope (e.g., drop calendar-booking integration, keep WhatsApp response layer only) — do not simply discount the same deliverable
- **Pricing strategy for first clients:** Price at the low end of each range to reduce friction and secure a testimonial/case study, rather than optimizing for maximum revenue per deal

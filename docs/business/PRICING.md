# Pricing

## Purpose

Document RelayOps' current prices, deal structure, and the strategic logic behind each choice.

---

## Background

Pricing was established during a mid-June 2026 session and has not yet been tested against a real closed deal. The strategy prioritizes friction reduction for the first several clients over revenue maximization.

---

## Current State

Prices are established and in use for outreach messaging. No pricing page exists on the website yet. No formal proposal template has been built.

---

## Price List

| Service | Price Range |
|---|---|
| Lead follow-up automation | $300–600 |
| Simple AI chatbot (website) | $400–800 |
| WhatsApp automation | $300–500 |
| Appointment booking system | $400–700 |
| Combined / multi-automation | $700–1,500 |

**General range used in outreach:** $300–800 per project.

All pricing is **project-based**, not subscription or retainer. No recurring pricing model has been discussed or decided.

---

## Deal Structure

- **50% deposit** to begin work
- **50% on delivery and satisfaction**
- **Delivery target:** 5–7 business days from deposit receipt
- **Scope-down rule:** If a prospect pushes back on price, reduce scope — do not simply discount the same deliverable. Example: drop the calendar-booking integration; keep only the WhatsApp response layer.

---

## Pricing Strategy

**Price at the lower end for the first several clients.** The goal is to minimize friction, close a deal, and collect a real testimonial/case study. A lower price with a happy client is worth more than a higher price with a slow-moving prospect.

**Do not negotiate on scope while keeping price fixed.** That creates delivery risk and sets a bad precedent. Reduce what's built; charge what it's worth.

**Increase prices after credibility exists.** Once real client results are documented, pricing should move up. No specific thresholds have been set for when this happens.

---

## What Doesn't Exist Yet

- No formal proposal template
- No pricing tiers or packages
- No pricing page on the website
- No retainer or ongoing-support pricing
- No discussion of when/how to transition to higher prices post-client-one

---

## Decisions

- Pricing established as project-based, not subscription/retainer (implicitly decided, June 2026 — no formal `DECISIONS.md` entry yet)
- Scope-down rule established rather than discounting (same session)
